Part 1

1 Getting started

You'll need a phone for yourself and a phone for each child, TaskKidz installed on all of them, and about 15 minutes.

Create your parent account

  1. Download TaskKidz and sign up with your name, email and a password.
  2. Create your family and give it a name. Your family code appears in the app straight away — your kids use it to join.

Add your child (on the child's phone)

  1. Install TaskKidz on your child's phone and open it.
  2. Choose to join a family as a kid.
  3. Your child enters their name, your family code, and picks a PIN they'll remember.
  4. This sends a join request to you.

Approve your child (on your phone)

You'll get your child's request to join — as a notification and as a card on your dashboard. Approve it, and they're in your family.

Turn on the phone controls (on the child's phone) — the important bit

This is what lets TaskKidz gently lock your child's phone when chores are overdue — and it's the one step worth doing carefully. As soon as your child signs in and TaskKidz sees they're a kid, it walks them through it:

Don't skip any of these If any control is left off, the locking may not work properly. The app shows a "Finish setup" checklist so nothing gets missed — work through every item until each shows a tick.

Two pop-ups appear first:

  1. "Activate device admin app" — a short description with buttons at the bottom (Cancel / Uninstall / Activate). Tap Activate. (Your phone may list what device admin can do — this is standard. TaskKidz only uses it to lock the screen when a chore is overdue.)
  2. "Allow TaskKidz to send you notifications" — tap Allow.

Then the kid dashboard opens with a "Finish setup" checklist. Two items are already ticked (Phone control and Notifications, from the pop-ups above). For each of the rest, tap Enable, which opens a settings screen — turn the TaskKidz switch on, then come back:

Set your payday and allowance

  1. Choose a payday — the day your weekly cycle resets.
  2. Set an allowance for each child, per week.
How the allowance works Each child's weekly allowance is split across their tasks. A daily task earns its share every day it's done; a weekly task earns its share once. So a bigger allowance shared over more chores means each one is worth a little; fewer chores means each is worth more. Decide your tasks first, then the allowance divides across them.
Good to know: once your child completes a task, that reward is locked in for them — if you change things later, an already-earned reward won't change. Changes to the allowance take effect from the next pay cycle once your child has started earning that week.

Create your tasks

  1. Create as many tasks as you like for each child — daily or weekly, each with a due time. New tasks start life as drafts.
  2. When you're ready, activate them from your dashboard — one at a time, or all at once.
Tasks start tomorrow Once you activate your tasks, they become active from the next day — so don't worry if they don't appear on your child's phone right away. Tip: set up all your tasks before activating, since the allowance splits across them.

Bonus tasks — extra jobs, up for grabs

A bonus task is an extra job that isn't tied to any one child. When you create one — say "Vacuum the lounge — $5" or "Wash my car — $10" — every child in the family gets a notification that a bonus task is available. The first child to accept it gets the job, and it disappears for everyone else.

Bonus money is on top: a bonus task pays a flat reward you set — it's separate from the weekly allowance, so it's genuinely extra pocket money for pitching in.

That's it — you're running

Your kids now see their tasks, do them, and submit them (with a photo if you like). You approve, and the reward lands in their balance. The next section explains exactly how the locking, bedtimes and payments work.

Part 2

2 How it all works

Everything below is here so nothing surprises you. Skim it now, or come back when a question comes up.

The locking — the heart of TaskKidz

  • A task is due at, say, 1:00 pm. If it isn't done, your child's phone locks.
  • When your child marks the task as done, the phone unlocks straight away — it wouldn't be fair to keep them locked while they wait for you to check it.
  • When you approve the task, great — the phone stays unlocked and the reward is paid.
  • If you tap Redo, the phone locks again and the task goes back to "to do" for your child to complete properly.
  • If you do nothing, the phone simply stays unlocked — until a bedtime cut-off kicks in or another task falls overdue.
What happens if a task just doesn't get done Say your child skips a task, the phone locks, and they head to bed. At midnight that overdue task clears — so the phone is no longer locked over it and your child starts the new day fresh. The catch: because the task wasn't done, its share of the allowance simply isn't earned — it's not carried over or given to anyone else. Do the chore, earn the share; skip it, and that little bit is missed.

Bedtimes — school nights, late nights and wake-ups

You can set the phone to lock itself at night and unlock in the morning. It's split into two kinds of night so weekdays and weekends can be different — and all of it is optional (skip it entirely if you'd rather not).

  • School Nights — an earlier auto-lock cut-off, for nights before school.
  • Late Nights — a later cut-off, or no cut-off at all, for weekends and holidays.
  • Wake Time — when the phone automatically unlocks again in the morning.
  • Day assignments — mark each day of the week as a School Night or a Late Night, so (for example) Sunday–Thursday lock early and Friday–Saturday stay open later.
Cut-off times run from early evening up to midnight, and wake times from early morning onwards — pick what suits your family, or leave them off.

Holiday mode

Turn on Holiday — for the whole family or just one child — and nothing will lock their phone. The locking simply pauses until Holiday is switched back off.

"I'll do this one" — doing a task for your child

Under any of your child's tasks on your phone, there's a green "I'll do this one" button. Tap it and the task is marked done: your child still gets the reward, and their phone won't lock over it. Handy when you'd rather just handle a chore yourself — or when your child isn't feeling well or has had a rough day.

Manual lock — for when they're not listening

On your phone, per child, you can manually lock their phone — for example if they're acting up. Your child can still complete their tasks and reach you while it's locked, and you unlock it back to normal whenever you're ready.

Savings goals

Kids can set a savings goal and watch a progress bar fill as their balance grows toward it — a nice way to encourage them to work for something bigger. It's a target and tracker; it doesn't lock their money away.

Your child's balance and paying them (the Pay tab)

  • Your child's balance is the money they've earned. It's held in your dashboard — there's no automatic payout, so you pay when they ask.
  • Pay all: under your child's name, tap Pay all — you pay the whole balance and it resets to $0.
  • Pay part: your child asks for, say, $12 — enter the amount, and the balance shows the remainder (e.g. $35 becomes $23).
  • Payday is just a label for the weekly cycle — no money moves automatically in the app.

Messages — and what works on the lock screen

  • In-app messages are only between a child and a parent — never between siblings.
  • Even when the phone is locked, your child can still complete tasks and call or message you. They're never cut off from reaching you.

The Task tab

See every task assigned to each child. This is also where you set each child's weekly allowance and choose your payday.

The Settings tab

  • Display name — what your kids see you as on their phones.
  • Phone number — used for your child's "call parent" button.
  • Change email — update your account email address.
  • App security — turn on Face ID / fingerprint unlock.
  • View goals — see what your kids are saving toward.
  • Reset a PIN — if a child forgets theirs.
  • Remove a child — take a child out of the family.
  • Danger Zone — permanently delete your whole family account and all its data. This can't be undone.

Your kids can make it theirs

In the kid app, your child picks an avatar and a colour. These show up on your phone and colour-code that child's tasks — an easy way to tell everyone's chores apart at a glance.

Notifications

TaskKidz keeps everyone in the loop with notifications — your child gets a reminder about 1 hour before a task is due, and you'll be notified when a task is approved, when a child asks to join, when a bonus task is grabbed, and more.

? Quick answers

The phone isn't locking

Check the phone controls are still on — open the "Finish setup" checklist on your child's phone and make sure every item has a tick, especially Phone control.

My child didn't get their task

New tasks are drafts until you activate them, and then they start the next day. Activate today, and they appear on your child's phone tomorrow.

How do I pay my child?

Go to the Pay tab, tap your child's name, and choose Pay all or enter a specific amount.
