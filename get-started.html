Part 1

A Getting started

You'll need a phone for yourself and a phone for each child, TaskKidz installed on all of them, and about 15 minutes.

1. Create your parent account

  1. Download TaskKidz and sign up with your name, email and a password.
  2. Create your family and give it a name. This generates a family code — your kids use it to join.
At signup, does the parent name the family and get the code right away, or is the code shown later? Want the wording exact.

2. Add your child (on the child's phone)

  1. Install TaskKidz on your child's phone and open it.
  2. Choose to join a family as a kid.
  3. Your child enters their name, your family code, and picks a PIN they'll remember.
  4. This sends a join request to you.

3. Approve your child (on your phone)

You'll get your child's request to join. Approve it, and they're in your family.

Is the approval a push notification, a card on the parent dashboard, or both?

4. Turn on the phone controls (on the child's phone) — the important bit

This is what lets TaskKidz gently lock your child's phone when chores are overdue — and it's the one step worth doing carefully. As soon as your child signs in and TaskKidz sees they're a kid, it walks them through it:

Don't skip any of these If any control is left off, the locking may not work properly. The app shows a "Finish setup" checklist so nothing gets missed — work through every item until each shows a tick.

Two pop-ups appear first:

  1. "Activate device admin app" — a short description with buttons at the bottom (Cancel / Uninstall / Activate). Tap Activate. (Your phone may show a list of what device admin can do — this is standard. TaskKidz only uses it to lock the screen when a chore is overdue.)
  2. "Allow TaskKidz to send you notifications" — tap Allow.

Then the kid dashboard opens with a "Finish setup" checklist. Two items are already ticked (Phone control and Notifications, from the pop-ups above). For each of the rest, tap Enable, which opens a settings screen — turn the TaskKidz switch on, then come back:

5. Set your payday and allowance

  1. Choose a payday — the day your weekly cycle resets.
  2. Set an allowance for each child, per week.
Important — how the allowance works A child's weekly allowance is split across their tasks. So if you set a $15/week allowance and give that child only one task, that single task is worth the whole $15. Decide your tasks first, then the allowance divides across them.

  1. Create tasks for each child — daily or weekly, each with a due time.
  2. Set all your tasks before activating them, because the allowance splits across them.

7. That's it — you're running

Your kids now see their tasks, do them, and submit them (with a photo if you like). You approve, and the reward lands in their balance. The next section explains exactly how the locking, limits and payments work.

Part 2

B How it all works

Everything below is here so nothing surprises you. Skim it now, or come back when a question comes up.

The locking — the heart of TaskKidz

  • A task is due at, say, 1:00 pm. If it isn't done, your child's phone locks.
  • When your child marks the task as done, the phone unlocks straight away — it wouldn't be fair to keep them locked while they wait for you to check it.
  • When you approve the task, great — the phone stays unlocked and the reward is paid.
  • If you tap Redo, the phone locks again and the task goes back to "to do" for your child to complete properly.
After the kid submits and the phone unlocks, if the parent does nothing at all, does it stay unlocked indefinitely, or re-lock after some time?

Limits — bedtimes and wake-up times

You can set cut-off times so the phone locks for the night, and wake times so it unlocks again in the morning — with different settings for school nights and other nights.

Structure of limits: separate school-night vs weekend/late-night cut-offs? Are wake times per-day or a single setting? Want to describe the real controls.

Holiday mode

Turn on Holiday and nothing will lock your child's phone — chores can pause and the phone stays free.

Is Holiday family-wide or per-kid? Does it pause tasks too, or only stop the locking?

"I'll do this one" — doing a task for your child

Under any of your child's tasks on your phone, there's a green "I'll do this one" button. Tap it and the task is marked done: your child still gets the reward, and their phone won't lock over it. Handy when you'd rather just handle a chore yourself.

Manual lock — for when they're not listening

On your phone, per child, you can manually lock their phone — for example if they're acting up. When you're ready, you unlock it back to normal.

While manually locked, can the kid still complete tasks to unlock, or is a manual lock "hard" until the parent unlocks? Important distinction for the guide.

Savings goals

Kids can set a savings goal and save toward it — a nice way to encourage them to work for something bigger.

Does saving toward a goal move money out of their spendable balance, or is the goal just a target/tracker sitting on top of the balance?

Your child's balance and paying them (the Pay tab)

  • Your child's balance is the money they've earned. It's held in your dashboard — there's no automatic payout, so you pay when they ask.
  • Pay all: under your child's name, tap Pay all — you pay the whole balance and it resets to $0.
  • Pay part: your child asks for, say, $12 — enter the amount, and the balance shows the remainder (e.g. $35 becomes $23).
  • Payday is just a label for the weekly cycle — no money moves automatically in the app.

Messages — and what works on the lock screen

  • In-app messages are only between a child and a parent — never between siblings.
  • Even when the phone is locked, your child can still complete tasks and call or message you. They're never cut off from reaching you.

The Task tab

See every task assigned to each child. This is also where you set each child's weekly allowance and choose your payday.

The Settings tab

  • Display name — what your kids see you as on their phones.
  • Phone number — used for your child's "call parent" button.
  • Change email — update your account email address.
  • App security — turn on Face ID / fingerprint unlock.
  • View goals — see what your kids are saving toward.
  • Reset a PIN — if a child forgets theirs.
  • Remove a child — take a child out of the family.
  • Danger Zone — permanently delete your whole family account and all its data. This can't be undone.

Your kids can make it theirs

In the kid app, your child picks an avatar and a colour. These show up on your phone and colour-code that child's tasks — an easy way to tell everyone's chores apart at a glance.

Notifications

Your child gets a reminder about 1 hour before a task is due, so there's time to get it done before the phone locks.

The phone isn't locking

Check the phone controls are still on — open the "Finish setup" checklist on your child's phone and make sure every item has a tick, especially Phone control.

My child didn't get their task

New tasks start the next day. Create it today, and it appears on your child's phone tomorrow.

How do I pay my child?

Go to the Pay tab, tap your child's name, and choose Pay all or enter a specific amount.
